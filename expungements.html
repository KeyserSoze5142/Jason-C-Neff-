Tuscaloosa Expungement Lawyer

Record sealing attorney — clear your record, protect your future.

A prior arrest or conviction on a criminal charge can be more than embarrassing. It can also prevent you from obtaining a job, renting an apartment, or being accepted at certain schools and colleges.

Expungement — Even if you were arrested and released without charges, or the charges were later dismissed, you will have a public record. In Alabama, you may be able to expunge these public records. With an expungement, the record is physically destroyed, and no one, not even law enforcement officials, will ever see it.

When a record is “expunged,” the court eliminates references to the charge from court records, official records, and police records. Police and courts consider them to never have happened, and generally will respond to any inquiry that no record exists about the matter.

When your record is expunged, you do not have to disclose expunged charges on credit, employment, or other types of applications!

Neff’s Top Ten Questions for Expungement

1. Can I get my record expunged if I have a felony?
Anyone charged with a non-violent felony may petition. A petition must be filed in circuit court in the county where charges were filed. This can be done in the following circumstances: (1) the charge was dismissed with prejudice, no-billed by a grand jury, or the person was found not guilty; OR (2) the charge was dismissed after successful completion of a drug court program, mental health court program, diversion program, veteran’s court, or any court-approved deferred prosecution program, one year after successful completion; OR (3) the charge was dismissed without prejudice more than 5 years ago, has not been re-filed, and the person has not been convicted of any other felony or misdemeanor crime, any violation, or any traffic violation (excluding minor traffic violations) during the previous five years; OR (4) 90 days have passed from the date of dismissal with prejudice, no-bill, acquittal, or nolle prosequi and the charge has not been re-filed.
2. Can I get my record expunged if I have a misdemeanor?
Anyone charged with a misdemeanor criminal offense, a violation, a traffic violation, or a municipal ordinance violation may petition in circuit court in the county where charges were filed if: (1) the charge was dismissed with prejudice, no-billed by grand jury, or the person was found not guilty; OR (2) the charge was dismissed without prejudice more than two years ago, has not been re-filed, and the person has not been convicted of any other felony or misdemeanor crime, any violation, or any traffic violation (excluding minor traffic violations) during the previous two years.
3. How can I go about getting my arrest removed from my permanent record?
You will need: (1) a sworn statement by the person seeking expungement confirming the requirements of the act are satisfied and whether you have applied before; (2) a certified record of arrest, disposition, or case action summary from the appropriate agency, along with a certified criminal record from the Alabama Criminal Justice Information Center; (3) specification of the criminal charges to be expunged and the arresting agency; and (4) service on the District Attorney, the arresting law enforcement agency, and the circuit clerk. The District Attorney will review and make reasonable efforts to notify the victim (if any). The District Attorney and the victim have 45 days to file a written objection; after 45 days with no objection, the objection is deemed waived.
4. Does the District Attorney have to agree for my charges to be expunged?
No, but if an objection is filed, the court sets a hearing no sooner than 14 days from the filing of the objection. The hearing is conducted according to the Alabama Rules of Evidence. The court considers: the nature and seriousness of the offense, the circumstances and date of the offense, the age of the offender at the time, whether it was an isolated or repeated incident, any probation or parole record, whether the offense was dismissed as part of a plea agreement, evidence of rehabilitation, and any other matter the court deems relevant. The judge must be “reasonably satisfied” that you have complied with the Expungement Act.
5. What does it mean if expungement is granted?
The court orders expungement of all records in the custody of the court and any other agency or official, including law enforcement records (except reports and case files held by the District Attorney and the Office of Parole Services), within 180 days of the order. After an order is entered, the court actions regarding the prior charge are deemed to have never happened.
6. What can I tell potential employers?
You do NOT have to disclose the record or any matter relating to it on applications for employment, credit, apartment rental, or other applications. HOWEVER, you have a duty to disclose the record to any government regulatory or licensing agency, any utility and its agents and affiliates, and a bank or other financial institution shall have the right to inspect the expunged records after filing notice with the court.
7. What does “record(s)” mean?
Arrest records, booking or arrest photographs, index references such as the State Judicial Information System or other governmental index references for public records search, or other data — whether in documentary or electronic form — relating to the arrest or charge.
8. What if I have restitution or court costs outstanding?
No order of expungement shall be granted unless all terms and conditions, including restitution, are satisfied and paid in full, including interest, to any victim or the Alabama Crime Victims’ Compensation Commission, as well as court costs, fines, or statutory fees ordered by the sentencing court, absent a finding of indigence by the court.
9. What if I lost my right to possess a firearm, but the record was expunged?
Expungement does NOT entitle an individual to ship, transport, possess, or receive a firearm. You still have to go through the Alabama Board of Pardons and Paroles.
10. Can I have my mug shot removed from mugshots.com, jailbase.com, etc.?
Once you retain the services of Neff, LLC, your chance of seeking removal from these databases goes up. One reason is that it is illegal for someone to expose or continue to expose your record once it has been expunged — they could be found guilty of a Class B misdemeanor.
